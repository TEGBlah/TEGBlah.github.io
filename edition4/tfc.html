Time for Crime

by Sansskrithi
═══════

Genre – Crime Thriller/Mystery

The day started good…… it was a pleasant day in the month of November. I was travelling to my mum’s place for Thanksgiving. Knowing a feast was waiting I was eagerly wanting to reach there without knowing what was afoot. I got of my plane in the Luis Muñoz Marín International Airport and took a car to my parent’s house in the Puerto Rico.

Before I continue any further let me tell a little bit about my family. I had my mum who lived in a beach house in Puerto Rico and was so loving and caring towards our family, a brother who is the smartest person I have met, my sister-in-law who is basically my own sister and their cute little daughter who was less than a year and had caught our heart with her cuteness, and finally the pillar and the oldest member of my family, my paternal grandmother who is 98 years old who live a few streets away my parent’s house. And of course, how can I forget our dog which my mom had and were always a part of my family.

So back to the story, I was travelling in car to reach my mum's place. Though the journey from the airport to the house was a long one it was also salubrious journey with palm trees at our eyesight and beautiful shores and blue water at a distance.

I was travelling from Nevada while my brother’s family was coming from California. We had planned to meet at our parents place the day before thanksgiving that was 24th November. Both of our planes were supposed to arrive at 3 pm but my plane got delayed due to technical issues. Hence, he had got to the house before me.

After a dreadfully tedious journey I was really happy to finally be home. I got of the car only to see that my brother’s family was waiting in the front lawn. I walked towards them to ask why they were waiting there. I asked and they answered “the door is locked outside and we she has gone out to get groceries. And I think even our cook, nurse and the maid have taken the day off because of thanksgiving. So we were wondering if you would have any spare keys?” and I replied “Sure I have one, will open the door, but instead of waiting you could have asked our neighbor as they always have one”

I opened the door and to my awful surprise I found my mom dead on the floor. All of us rushed down to the hall, but unfortunately, we were too late. She was dead before we got there.
